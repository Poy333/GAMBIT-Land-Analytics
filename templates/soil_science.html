TYPES OF LAND SUITABLE AND NOT SUITABLE FOR FARMING

Understanding the types of land that can support agriculture is essential for effective land use planning. Farming success depends heavily on soil quality, topography, climate, and water availability. Generally, land can be classified into two broad categories: suitable for farming and not suitable for farming.

Suitable for Farming

Not Suitable for Farming

Knowing what kind of land you have is very important for farming. Using land that is good for growing crops—and fixing or avoiding land that isn’t—can help make farming more successful and long-lasting.

We, The GAMBIT, are here to help you identify land in the mountain barangays of Cebu City that is suitable for farming, and also show you which areas are not good for planting.
