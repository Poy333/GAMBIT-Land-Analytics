WHAT WE DO?

We assist farmers in identifying and allocating suitable land for agriculture, conducting thorough surveys, and providing a range of support services to enhance productivity and sustainability.

Land Identification Workshops

We conduct practical workshops where farmers and local communities are trained to identify potential land suitable for farming. These workshops emphasize using geographic tools and soil analysis to assess land quality and suitability for various crops.

Soil Quality Surveys

Our team works closely with local farmers to survey land and analyze soil conditions. This data is used to help identify areas that are ideal for farming and those that require interventions to improve soil health.

Mapping & Data Analysis for Agricultural Land Use

We gather geographic data, including topography and climate, to help farmers understand which areas are the best for farming. Using this information, we create detailed maps and analysis that guide land use planning and crop decisions.

Community Collaboration

Our research is not only about finding land for farming but also about creating a community-driven approach to sustainable land use. By working together with local communities, we ensure that our findings are aligned with their needs and environmental conditions.
